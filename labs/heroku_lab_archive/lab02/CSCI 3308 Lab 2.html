PART A: Section 1. Meet Your Team

(15 Minutes)

1. Find your group members
Gather everyone on your team and arrange yourselves in a small circle (in this case we'll be adding all of you to a breakout room). Make sure your audio and/or video are functional so that everyone can see and hear all group members.
You should have received an invite to a GitHub repository for the group project. Accept the invite right away. The invite link expires within 7 days.
2. Assign a SCRIBE
You should pick one person to document today's lab in a MS Word doc your Google doc. You will show your documentation to your TA at the end of lab AND your team will upload a single PDF to Canvas at the end of lab time. Make sure everyone's name is on this document.
3. Introductions
Go around in a circle, state your name, home town, major, year in school, and where in the world you would like to go for a three-week "dream" vacation after the end of this semester (this is hypothetical so pick anything).
After all team members have introduced themselves, each team member should go around in a circle and state the names of the other team members. It is okay to ask your team members for help.

The team scribe should document everyone's name.
4. Team name
As a team, decide on a team name. This can be anything but must be appropriate (i.e., no profanity, etc.).

The team scribe should write down your team name.
5. GitHub usernames
Go around in a circle and document each group member's GitHub username.

The team scribe should write each person's GitHub username next to their name.
6. Schedule a meeting time with your Team
As a team, decide on a weekly meeting time and place to work on your group project.
  • You MUST pick a time outside of class/recitation.
  • All team members MUST be able to attend the meeting. Pick a time that works for EVERYONE.
  • You MUST meet for two hours each week. This can be one 2-hour meeting or two 1-hour meetings.
  • Think about where you might meet. You don't have to decide this right now, but come up with some options that work for everyone.
  • Create a meeting invite on a platform of your choosing and share that link with your TA.
The team scribe should document your weekly meeting time.
7. Schedule a meeting time with your TA
You will be participating in a standup meeting with your TA every week. This meeting will be short, approximately 10 minutes. During this meeting, you will show the TA your progress towards completing the project and have an opportunity to ask questions and get feedback.
  • You MUST pick a time outside of class/recitation.
  • All team members MUST be able to attend the meeting. Pick a time that works for EVERYONE.
  • You MUST meet for 20 minutes each week.
  • One of your team members will be presenting the team's progress. You may choose to make slides, but it is not required. We expect each team member to be working on development of the project every week.
  • You will meet your TA on Zoom. The TA will setup a Zoom meeting for you.
The team scribe should document the weekly meeting time with the TA.

PART A: Section 6. Planning Poker

(15 Minutes)

As a team, you will decide how difficult it will be to implement a given User Story in terms of Story Points. Story Points are an abstract measure of the effort required to implement a User Story. In this step, you will assign Story Points to your user story using a process called 'Planning Poker'.

Planning Poker: Planning poker is a game wherein each individual team member proposes Story Points for a given User Story, the team discusses each others proposals, and collaborativality assigns story points to a given User Story.
You can watch a short video on how to play Planning Poker here.
1. Create your cards
Each individual team member should create a set of cards numbered 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, and 13. You can use sticky notes or pieces of paper. Set these cards in front of you.
2. Discuss the User Story diffculty
As a team, discuss how difficult you think the User Story will be to implement. How much time do you think it will take to code? Do you anticipate any challenges with APIs or libraries?
3. Choose the number of Story Points
In secret, each individual will choose a Story Point to assign to the given Story. It is important that each team member chooses in secret so that they do not influence another team member's choice. On the count of 3, each team member will hold up the Story Point that they think is most appropriate given how difficult they think the User Story is to implement.
4. Come to a consensus
* Did everyone choose the same value or close to the same value? Great, you all agree and you can assign the choosen value to the User Story.
* Did some individuals pick significantly higher values than others? Why? Are there anticipated challenges? Discuss your differences in opinion and come to a consensus. You may have to convince someone to change their rating.
5. Assign Story Points
In Jira, click on your User Story. A sidebar will appear. In the sidebar, click on 'Story Points' and assign your choosen value. You may have to scroll to see the 'Story Points' form field.
